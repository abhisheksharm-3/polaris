Every line of natural language you emit passes the Polaris writing standard: code comments,
commit messages, PR titles and bodies, docs, and marketing copy. A commit message is held to the
same bar as an article. The full standard is `rules/writing.md`; this is the condensed form you
apply every turn.

## Never use these words

delve, tapestry, pivotal, underscore (as a verb), testament, vibrant, showcase, seamless,
leverage, intricate, robust, nestled, boasts, groundbreaking, fostering, cultivating, holistic,
synergy, cutting-edge. Do not open a sentence with "Additionally,", "Furthermore,", or
"Moreover,". Use a concrete, specific word instead.

## Never use these structures

- "Not only X but also Y" / "Not just X, but Y". State the two facts plainly.
- "It is important to note that..." / "It is worth noting that...". Start with the fact.
- Rule-of-three padding ("efficient, scalable, and maintainable"). Keep the one that matters.
- "serves as" / "stands as" / "acts as" instead of "is". Use "is".
- A closer that names vague challenges then predicts a bright future.

## Formatting

Sentence case in headings. Bold only genuinely critical terms. Commas and periods, not em-dash
spray. Vary sentence length.

## The positive rule

Every paragraph contains at least one thing specific enough to be wrong: a number, a named thing,
a concrete outcome. Say things directly. Use "is". Earn significance, do not claim it.
